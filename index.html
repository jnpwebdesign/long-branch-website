Long Branch Historic House and Farm

Come enjoy the beauty, learn the history, and experience the offerings of the Long Branch Historic House and Farm. An idyllic setting for humans and animals alike, there are over 400 rolling acres to provide for relaxing equine retirement as well as a scenic wedding venue in Northern Virginia. We invite you to enjoy a picnic in our scenic gardens, walk the pristine grounds, or enjoy one of our many on-site events.

The History and Present of Long Branch

Beginning with land inherited by Robert Carter Burwell in 1788, Long Branch began as a wheat plantation, and was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1969. Read more about its long history, beginning with the Native Americans who lived on the land prior to English settlement, to the enslaved people who built and worked the plantation, to its current use mission of supporting the Shenandoah Valley community.

Rent Our Property for Weddings & Private Events

Nestled against the foothills of the Blue Ridge Mountains, Long Branch boasts some of the best views of the Shenandoah Valley. Our Sheila Macqueen garden is an ideal space for traditional Virginia weddings as it surrounds a beautifully groomed ceremony site. For smaller parties or inclement weather, the Main Gallery of the mansion can accommodate up to 75 guests for a more intimate Virginia wedding and reception venue. Regardless of the location you choose, Long Branch offers a Bridal Suite and Groom's Room to prepare and relax for your big day. The Bridal Suite, with views of the ceremony site, is a gorgeous upstairs room with elegant dressing room and bathroom for the bride and her bridesmaids to get ready, while the groom's “Tack Room” is an ideal spot for the groom and his groomsmen to enjoy pre-wedding preparations.
